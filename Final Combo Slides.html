Conclusion

Japan's demographic shifts—such as environmental concentration, population aging, and regional inequality—are deeply interconnected with its geography. Through these geospatial visualizations, we observed how over 90% of the population clusters in a small fraction of the land, while rural areas face depopulation and housing vacancy.

By combining demographic data with cartography, we can better understand Japan’s challenges and opportunities—from designing livable cities to addressing the social effects of a shrinking population. These insights show the power of maps not just to represent space, but to tell the evolving story of a nation.
